Transparency

AI is a tool. Judgment is ours.

We're a finance advisory firm that uses AI to move faster on the work that doesn't require judgment, and slower on the work that does. This page covers what AI does in our workflow, what we don't let it do, how we handle your data, and which models we use.

01

What AI does in our workflow

AI helps us compress the time between data and insight. The faster we get to a clean draft, the more time we spend on what matters: judgment, context, and what you should do next.

  • Categorization and tagging. Bank lines, expense lines, GL entries. AI proposes categories. A senior reviewer signs off.
  • First-draft commentary. Variance explanations, MIS narratives, board-pack write-ups. AI drafts from your numbers. We edit, fact-check, and add the context AI cannot know.
  • Reconciliation acceleration. Pattern matching across statements, ledgers, and invoices. AI flags mismatches. We investigate.
  • Analysis acceleration. Cohort cuts, segment math, scenario builds. AI handles the mechanical work. We interpret.
02

What humans always do

These are the parts we never delegate to AI. Not because AI cannot do them, but because the value of finance advisory comes from the judgment behind each decision.

  • Review and sign-off on every deliverable. Nothing leaves SK Capital without a senior finance professional reading it line by line.
  • Client-facing communication. Calls, emails, meetings, advice. You talk to people, not models.
  • Judgment calls. What's a real cost cut versus a false economy. Whether a number means something. Whether a forecast assumption holds.
  • Anomaly investigation. When something looks off, a human digs in. AI is a starting point, not an answer.
03

How we handle your data

Finance data is sensitive. We treat it that way.

  • NDAs by default. Standard mutual NDA before we look at any number.
  • No training on your data. We use enterprise and API access modes that explicitly opt out of model training. Your numbers do not become training data for an external model.
  • Data minimization. We send only what's needed for the task. Where we can work on a redacted or aggregated version, we do.
  • Logging and retention. We keep audit trails of AI calls for our own quality control. We do not retain prompts or outputs beyond the engagement.
  • Data residency. For clients with specific residency needs (EU, US, India, UAE, Singapore, Australia), we configure our stack to meet them. Ask before we start, not after.
04

Models we use

We pick the model that fits the task. Currently:

  • Claude (Anthropic). Drafting, reasoning, long-context summarization, deliverable review.
  • GPT-class models (OpenAI, Azure). Categorization, structured extraction, code-assisted analysis.
  • Specialized tooling. OCR for scanned statements, vector search for past-deliverable retrieval within an engagement.
Models change. Principles do not. We update this page as the stack evolves. If the change matters for clients, we tell them directly.
05

What you can ask us

If you ever want to know which parts of a deliverable involved AI assistance, ask. We will tell you.

  • Which model touched what. We will name the model used on a specific task.
  • What AI did versus what a human did. Line by line if you want it that way.
  • The prompt patterns we used. If you want to reproduce or audit them, we'll share.
  • Honest labeling. We will not call something human-made if AI assisted, and we will not call something AI-made if a human shaped it.
